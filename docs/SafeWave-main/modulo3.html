MODULE 3 - Privacy in Browsing and Device Use

Objective: Use browsers and tools that respect user privacy, properly configure application permissions, and keep devices protected and up to date.

Privacy-focused browsers

Not all browsers treat your data the same way. Some collect information about your browsing habits to sell targeted advertising, while others prioritize protecting your privacy.

Recommended options:

  • Firefox with privacy settings: Blocks trackers by default and allows advanced settings for greater security.
  • Brave: Blocks ads and trackers by default, plus includes Tor browsing in private mode.
  • Tor Browser: Routes your connection through multiple nodes to make it harder to track your activity.

Why avoid browsers such as Chrome or Edge?

These browsers, although popular, are linked to companies that monetize user data (Google and Microsoft). If you use them, always check your privacy settings to disable tracking.

Using private/incognito browsing modes (and their limitations)

Incognito or private mode does not make your browsing invisible; it only prevents the browser from saving history, cookies, or passwords on your device.

What private mode does NOT do:

  • Hide your activity from your internet service provider (ISP).
  • Prevent the websites you visit from tracking your IP address.
  • Protect you from malware or phishing.

When to use it?

  • On shared devices (such as a public computer).
  • To prevent credentials or searches from being saved on your personal computer.

If you need more privacy, combine it with a VPN or the Tor browser.

Configuring app permissions on smartphones and tablets

Many apps request access to features such as the camera, microphone, or location without any real need. This can be a risk to your privacy.

Steps to review permissions (example on Android and iOS):

  1. Android: Settings ‚Üí Applications ‚Üí Permissions.
  2. iOS: Settings ‚Üí Privacy ‚Üí Select category (e.g., Camera).

Critical permissions you should control:

  • Location: Only for map or delivery apps.
  • Microphone and camera: Revoke access in apps such as social media if you don't use them.
  • Contacts and files: Avoid giving access to untrustworthy games or apps.

Tip: Periodically review which apps have permissions and disable those you don't use.

The importance of keeping your software and operating system up to date

Updates not only bring new features, but also patch vulnerabilities that could be exploited by hackers.

Examples of risks of not updating:

  • Attacks on old systems: The WannaCry ransomware affected computers running outdated versions of Windows.
  • Failures in outdated applications: Vulnerabilities in WhatsApp and Telegram have allowed espionage in the past.

What to do?

  • Enable automatic updates on your operating system.
  • Manually update your applications from time to time.
  • Do not ignore update notifications, especially on mobile devices.

Using VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) to protect your connection

A VPN encrypts your internet connection, hiding your IP address and preventing third parties (such as your ISP or public WiFi networks) from seeing your activity.

Cases where a VPN is useful:

  • When connecting to public Wi-Fi (coffee shops, airports).
  • To access geo-restricted content.
  • If you want to prevent your internet provider from logging your history.

Recommended options:

  • Free VPNs (with caution): ProtonVPN (limited in free version).
  • Most reliable paid VPNs: NordVPN, ExpressVPN, Mulwad.

Caution: Not all VPNs are secure. Avoid those that log your activity or have questionable privacy policies.

Device encryption and backups

Encryption: Converts your data into unreadable information without a key, protecting it in case of theft or loss of the device.

  • Android/iOS: Already include automatic encryption when using a PIN or fingerprint.
  • Windows/macOS: Enable BitLocker (Windows) or FileVault (macOS).

Backups:

  • In the cloud: iCloud, Google Drive, or encrypted services such as Tresorit.
  • Physically: External hard drives, but these must also be encrypted.

Why are they important?

  • If you lose your phone or your laptop is stolen, your files will be protected.
  • In the event of device failure, you will be able to recover your information.

Suggested Activities:

  • Device Review: Each participant reviews the permissions of three apps on their phone and adjusts them as necessary.
  • Mini-game: Interactive Privacy Configurator on the web.
